It is my great pleasure to present the full symposium program for the RDF session at the [American Chemical Society](http://www.acs.org/)
at the Boston meeting in August:

[http://egonw.github.com/acsrdf2010/](https://egonw.github.com/acsrdf2010/)

I am excited that on Monday afternoon [Eric Prud'hommeaux](http://esw.w3.org/Eric_Prud'hommeaux) will present the work of the
[LODD working group](http://esw.w3.org/HCLSIG/LODD) to the chemistry community. The symposium contains three half day sessions with
topics on computing, ontologies, and applications, all chemistry oriented. The goal of the meeting is to get together people using
RDF technologies in chemistry, and the list of talks from around the world shows that this goal has been reached. The program is
diverse and exciting, and I am very much looking forward to meeting all participants to discuss challenges and cool solutions.

![](/blog/assets/images/acsrdf.png)

People interested in joining, can sign up to the [meeting mailing list](https://groups.google.com/group/acsrdf2010). Besides
that the webpage is in [XHTML+RDFa](http://www.w3.org/TR/xhtml-rdfa-primer/), the source is also available on GitHub (well, you
really download the source code anyway), allowing people to [happily fork](http://egonw.github.com/acsrdf2010/#morerdfa), make
changes, and perhaps make the page as triple-dense as is possible.

I am also keen to have mastered some [jQuery](http://jquery.com/) skills, and the Abstract links on the webpage use jQuery to
show and hide them.
